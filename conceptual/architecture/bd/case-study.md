# Case 

---

Mr. Sebastião has three children: one aged 20, one aged 17, and 
another aged 14. For the 20-year-old son, it is easy for Mr.
Sebastião to transfer the monthly allowance because he already has
a bank account. However, the other children do not have bank
accounts due to their age, so their allowance has to be given
in cash, which is neither efficient nor safe.

For this reason, Mr. Sebastião contacted the group of programmers Akatsuke
and proposed the creation of a digital wallet. In this wallet, users must
provide their name, phone number, ID number, date of birth,
and a security PIN.

Users will be able to make transactions between themselves,
where each transaction contains the type of transaction,
the amount, and the date of the transaction.

For users under 18 years old, they must register with the assistance
of an adult representative. In this case, they must provide the
name of the adult representative, the ID number, and the transaction
limit.

Users will also be able to make payments to companies (such as recharging
Unitel/Africell balance, paying electricity bills, buying
tickets, etc.).

These companies must provide the company name, the NIF (tax identification
number), and a linked bank account.

A user may perform several transactions, and users will be able to
view their transaction history.
